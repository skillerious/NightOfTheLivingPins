Dress the level in controlled zones

Professional environment art is controlled. You are not filling space randomly - you are guiding the player's eye from the ball to the lane to the zombie pins.

Zone-based dressingHero focal point

Zone A - Approach

  • Ball return machine
  • Old carpet strips
  • Scattered bowling shoes
  • Broken chair
  • Dirty foul line

Zone B - Lane

  • Subtle scuffs
  • Wet reflective strips
  • Cracked boards near gutters
  • Aiming arrows visible
  • Clean centre path

Zone C - Side walls

  • Peeling posters
  • Lane number signs
  • Old score monitor cables
  • Mold and water stains
  • Emergency exit sign

Zone D - Pin deck

  • Low fog
  • Pin spot stains
  • Broken machine arms
  • Hanging cables
  • Glowing red/green warning light

Zone E - Back machine

  • Broken pinsetter
  • Metal jaws silhouette
  • Conveyor belt shapes
  • Steam leak
  • Zombie groan audio source

Zone F - Ceiling

  • Broken fluorescent tubes
  • Missing ceiling tiles
  • Water leaks
  • Hanging wires
  • Fan shadow movement

Prop placement workflow

  1. Find the prop in the Content Drawer or Fab window.
  2. Drag it into the viewport near the correct zone.
  3. Rename the actor if it is important for review or scripting.
  4. Use W, E, and R for rough placement, then clean up values in Details.
  5. Put the prop in 04_PROPS_DRESSING or the matching zone folder.

Selection sanity checks

When the scene gets crowded, select actors from the Outliner instead of hunting in the viewport. Use Outliner folders to hide side-wall clutter while checking the lane, then reveal everything for final screenshots.
