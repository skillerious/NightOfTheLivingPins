Make the lane spooky without making it unreadable

The lighting target is a readable golden-brown lane surrounded by cold darkness, with dirty green and red accents around the pin deck. The player should always know where to aim.

  • Keep the lane reflection visible.

Back-machine recipe

  • Add a Spot Light behind the pins aimed forward.
  • Use red/green contrast, not pure red everywhere.
  • Let the pins silhouette against fog and machinery.
  • This is your horror focal point.

How to add lights

  1. Open Place Actors > Lights.
  2. Drag Rect Light or Spot Light into the viewport.
  3. Rename it in the Outliner, then move it into 03_LIGHTING.
  4. Type Location, Rotation, Intensity, Source Width, and Source Height in Details.

How to aim the spotlight

Select SpotLight_BackMachine, press E for Rotate, and aim the cone toward the pin rack. For accuracy, type Rotation values in Details after rough aiming in the viewport.

How to judge the pass

Switch the viewport to Lit, press G for Game View, and take a screenshot from the approach. If the ball path or head pin disappears, reduce fog/bloom or raise lane key light.

Make it feel abandoned

Use long shadow shapes, dead corners, low ceiling pools of light, and one strong back glow. The best horror lighting leaves some parts unseen, but never hides the gameplay lane itself.

Keep the gameplay readable

The ball path needs to read immediately. Use the foul line, target arrows, rails, and a subtle lane reflection to guide the eye toward the pins.

Do not overdo dynamic lights

Use dynamic lighting where it matters: flicker lights, emissive monitors, scare moments, and the pin machine. Keep unnecessary small lights out of the prototype until the gameplay is proven.
