Add gameplay markers before you add final gameplay

Markers are the bridge between the level and your future C++ code. They let you spawn balls, pins, cameras, scare events, and enemies without hard-coding weird coordinates later.

Trigger volumes

  • TV_Gutter_L detects left gutter balls.
  • TV_Gutter_R detects right gutter balls.
  • TV_Pit detects when the ball reaches the back.
  • TV_ApproachArea enables pre-throw aiming and charging.
  • FoulLineVolume blocks the player from crossing during active bowling.

Target Point editor clicks

  1. Open Place Actors > Basic.
  2. Drag Target Point into the level.
  3. Rename it to the exact TP_ name.
  4. In Details, type the Location values from the table.
  5. Put pin points in 05_GAMEPLAY_MARKERS / PinSpots and camera/spawn points in matching subfolders.

Trigger and tag workflow

  1. Open Place Actors > Volumes for trigger or blocking volumes.
  2. Use Details > Transform to size the volume around the gameplay area.
  3. Use Details > Actor > Tags to add tags such as BallSpawn, PlayerView, FoulLine, CameraTarget, or ScarePoint.
  4. Use the Outliner eye button to temporarily hide big blockout pieces while checking volume placement.
